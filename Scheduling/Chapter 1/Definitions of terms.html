Scheduling System of Mandaue City College


DEFINITION OF TERMS

In order to provide clear interpretation of basic concept used in this study, the following terms are conceptually and operationally defined.


Accurate – Capable of providing results that are correct not making mistakes.

School – Provides quality general education to enable students to think critically, communicate effectively, complete accurately and adapt to change appropriately Operationally, in this study, it is the cooperating agency where the study was conducted.

Class Schedules – It refers to a specific subject, time, and room of a certain class.

Class Scheduler – known as college style scheduling, is a form of hand scheduling that uses information from the Master Schedule and produces cards or labels with the course information printed on them Operationally, as used in this study, it is the Automated Class scheduler proposed by the researchers.

Department Head – acts or presides as chair, as of an academic department in a university Operationally, the Department Head is a faculty member responsible for the daily operation of the program as well as long-term oversight of planning, scheduling, and curriculum development.

Efficiency – In mechanics, the measure of the effectiveness with which a system performs Operationally, it is used to measure the effectiveness of class schedule.

Instructors –The entire teaching staff of a university, college, or school, including any administrators holding academic rank ). Operationally, it refers to the faculty of [name of the school] who were one of the respondents of the study.

Students – Is primarily a person enrolled in a school or other educational institution and who is under learning with goals of acquiring knowledge, developing professions and achieving employment in a desired field.
